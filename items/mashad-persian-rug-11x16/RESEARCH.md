## What Is a Mashad Rug?

Mashhad (also spelled Mashad or Meshad) rugs are produced in the city of Mashhad in northeastern Iran (Khorasan province). They are among the most prestigious and widely collected Persian rugs, known for:

- **Large format** — Mashhad is one of the few Persian weaving centers that specializes in room-size and oversized carpets
- **Fine knotting** — typically high KPSI (knots per square inch) using Khorasan wool
- **Classic designs** — medallion-and-corner layouts, dense allover floral fields (the "Herati" or "mahi" pattern is common)
- **Wool pile on cotton foundation** — considered high quality construction

A "Fine" Mashad designation (as on the provenance letter) refers to a higher knot density and more detailed craftsmanship within the Mashhad category.

## Value Assessment

### The honest picture on resale vs. original purchase price

Persian rugs bought from retail dealers (especially in the US) carry significant dealer markup — often 3–5x actual market value. A rug sold retail for $24,000–$27,000 in 1999 does **not** necessarily have a $24K resale value today. This is the single most important thing to understand.

That said, **fine Mashhad rugs are genuinely valuable** and this one has significant positives going for it:

**Positives:**
- Large format (11'6" x 16") — a desirable, statement size
- "Fine" designation with provenance letter confirming authenticity
- Classic navy/floral design — broadly appealing
- 25+ years old — moving toward "vintage" status which helps value
- Receipt + letter from original dealer = strong provenance (rare and valuable)
- Mashhad is one of the most prestigious Persian rug origins

**Unknowns that affect value:**
- Knot density (KPSI) — needs hands-on inspection
- Condition: any wear, fading, repairs, moth damage, stains?
- Whether it's been professionally cleaned recently

## ⚠️ Important Note on eBay

eBay has restrictions on selling items **made in Iran** due to US trade embargo regulations. Persian rugs technically fall under this policy, though enforcement is inconsistent. Some sellers list them, many have listings removed. **Facebook Marketplace and Chairish do not have this restriction.** If listing on eBay, describe as "handmade Oriental/wool area rug" without explicitly calling it "Persian" or "Iranian."

## Recommended Next Steps (before listing)

1. **Get a professional appraisal** — A written appraisal from a certified rug appraiser ($75–$150) will: (a) confirm actual value, (b) serve as a selling tool, (c) help set a defensible price. Look for an ASA (American Society of Appraisers) or AAA certified appraiser.
2. **Professional cleaning** — A clean rug photographs and shows dramatically better. Expect $200–$400 for a rug this size. It increases perceived value significantly.
3. **Take more current photos** — The existing photos are mostly old snapshots. Need: full rug laid flat, close-up of center medallion, close-up of border, photo of the back (knots), and a photo showing the fringe condition.
4. **Consider platforms** — For fastest sale: FB Marketplace. For best price: Chairish or a local high-end consignment shop.
